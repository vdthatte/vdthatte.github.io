Wearhacks

October 2015



Wearhacks took place on one of the weekends of October, right before my data structures and linear algebra midterm. Obviously, in my head hacking > exams. So I decided to go to the hackathon and also pursuade my friends to come along. We formed a team of 5, Hieu, Jasleen, Sahir, Jeton and I. And as we brainstormed for ground breaking ideas while waiting in line for food, Hieu came up with the idea for Alertify. After 2 days of caffeine filled days and sleepless nights, Alertify was born. Alertify is a gesture sensing app for the android wear watch, which makes emergency calls to your loved ones based on gestures performed by the user. We used Presto's API to recognize gestures and attributed different gestures to different actions. The two primary actions were calling and texting coordinates. We used android's telephony api to perform the phone call through the user's cell phone and SMS api to send the emergency alert SMS. The coordinates of a person's location were also sent through SMS using Android's geolocation API. Oh, did I mention we won the best project under software category?
Checkout the project on challenge post.
